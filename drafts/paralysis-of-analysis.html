Paralysis of Analysis

Thu 01 August 2013

Since being involved in the creation of much larger and complex systems, I have taken a much slower and careful approach to the phases involved before development begins. I now view the planning, design, modeling and architecture of a system playing an equal or even greater part than the coding of the system itself.

However, I have noticed a recurring pattern emerging, especially as these systems become larger. One of my coworkers has alerted me that I have been entering a state known as analysis paralysis:

Analysis paralysis or paralysis of analysis is the state of over-analyzing (or over-thinking) a situation so that a decision or action is never taken, in effect paralyzing the outcome.

It is a very common situation especially for developers, but it occurs in all aspects of life. As I've been catching myself falling into this situation much more often, I knew I had to become better at two things: developing an awareness for when I have been paralyzed with choices for too long, and figuring out a way to get out of it.

So, what do you do when you have too many options?

Awareness

Like any other problem, becoming aware of its existence is always the first step.

When developing software, there are a few techniques and questions that I ask to help me identify when I have been in paralysis for too long:

  • When was the last time I was at a keyboard?
  • Have I been thinking about an implementation longer than it would have taken to just make it?
